//MOLLY BORIS

I am a poet and multimedia artist. I combine found images, personal photographs, and contemporary motifs using ink and paint to create symbols of my perceptions of beauty and gender roles. Rather than appealing to the sexes, I hope to just confuse them. Themes of feminism and absurdism are notable traits that can be seen in my work. I deconstruct the norms that oppress me, remove their weight, and implement a policy of abnormalities. I create a new concept of beauty that exists beyond our conditioned imagination. Iâ€™ve intertwined these subjects as a way to communicate my experiences as a woman. My work has been said to provoke thoughts about the body, sex, advertisement, and fetishism. I paint and print mostly in black and white, and I try to be economical with my use of color. I like to convey a dissociative blankness that swallows a subject matter. As a young radical, I have formed polarizing opinions about my own sex appeal and gender performance expectations. I draw my inspiration from my everyday interactions with other women, and from my internalized experiences with the men. My work is heavily influenced by the DADA movement, The Dadaists, and Yayoi Kusama. DADA made a mockery of human behavior. Kusama translated her obsessive and intrusive thoughts into visionary spaces. Photographs of the human body and its parts are essential components in my work that I have reinvented through assemblage as a response to the absurdity that I find in the standard of beauty and attraction. I consider my artwork to be a subconscious reflection of my own want to be desirable while simultaneously meeting conventional expectations with retaliation. I use my practice as a way of moving towards my own liberation. I have spent years of my life trying to decode the complexes of beauty that surround me. I observe deeply beyond the surfaces of my environment and take careful consideration of what it really means to experience and embody beauty.
